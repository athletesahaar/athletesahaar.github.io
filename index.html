Expert Nutrition Tips for Peak Performance

Tip 1: The Golden 30-Minute Window

Always consume a quick-acting carb and protein source (like a banana and a scoop of whey) within 30 minutes post-workout. This is when your muscles are most receptive to recovery, maximizing gains and minimizing soreness.

Tip 2: Hydration is NOT Just Water

For sessions lasting over 60 minutes, plain water isn't enough. You need electrolytes (sodium, potassium). Mix a pinch of sea salt into your water bottle or use a natural sports drink to maintain nerve and muscle function.

Tip 3: The Power of Sleep Fuel

A slow-digesting protein (like cottage cheese or casein powder) before bed prevents muscle breakdown overnight. Think of it as a low-level fuel drip for your muscles while you rest and repair.
